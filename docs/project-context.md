## 1. Problem Statement
Consumers who want to buy clean, non-toxic home and pantry products cannot do so efficiently because product labels are misleading, ingredient lists require expertise to interpret, and researching alternatives across multiple sources is slow and fragmented. The problem is compounded by the lack of any persistent place to store the preferences users build up over time.

## 2. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
Someone actively trying to replace toxic or chemically questionable products in their home — cleaning supplies, personal care items, pantry staples — who already cares about ingredient safety but lacks the time or expertise to evaluate products independently. They currently bounce between product labels, review sites, and databases like EWG without a unified workflow. They have accumulated opinions (brands they trust, ingredients they avoid) but no system to capture or apply them consistently.

## 3. Pain Points
- Product labels use scientific ingredient names that require expertise to evaluate, making self-research impractical
- "Clean," "natural," and "non-toxic" marketing claims are unverified and unreliable, creating distrust but no alternative signal
- Research is scattered across multiple sources (EWG, review sites, brand sites) with no way to synthesize results
- Preferences and knowledge accumulated over time — trusted brands, flagged ingredients — have no persistent home and must be re-applied from scratch with each purchase decision

## 4. Proposed Solution
A conversational agent (backed by Claude) through which users can:
- Describe what they're looking for and receive researched product recommendations with ingredient safety reasoning
- Save preferences — ingredients to avoid, trusted brands, preferred certifications (EWG Verified, USDA Organic, B Corp) — that are automatically applied to future recommendations
- Compare products side by side and receive a clear recommendation based on their saved preferences
- Add recommended products to a persistent shopping cart that carries across sessions
